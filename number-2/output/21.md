# Page 21

The partially collapsed entrance is tighter, but the fresh scratches in the dirt make you curious. Someone has been here recently. You squeeze through the opening, dust and small rocks cascading around you.

Inside, the tunnel is rougher than the main shaft—less of a proper mine, more like a natural cave that was widened. Your flashlight reveals fresh footprints in the dust. Your pulse quickens.

The tunnel curves sharply, then opens into a small chamber. You freeze. Someone has been camping here: a sleeping bag, water bottles, granola bar wrappers. And on the wall, pinned with a knife, is a piece of paper—a photocopy of a map that looks very similar to yours!

You hear a sound behind you—boots on stone. Someone else is in the tunnel.

"Hey!" a voice calls. "Who's there?"
