# Page 68

You wade carefully around the pool, keeping to the shallow edge. The water is cold and crystal clear. You can see the bottom—smooth stones worn by ancient water flow.

As you reach the far side, you approach the three chests. They're wooden, bound with iron, and look ancient. You kneel before the first chest and lift the lid.

Empty. Your heart sinks.

You check the second chest. Also empty.

The third chest—you lift the lid with fading hope. Empty as well.

You sit back, disappointed. Someone found this treasure long ago. But wait—inside the third chest, scratched into the wood: "Moved to safety. Look below."

Below? You examine the pool more carefully. In its center, you notice something you missed before—a darker patch on the bottom, like an opening.

You wade into the pool, testing the depth. At the center, it drops away—there's an underwater passage! The treasure must be hidden in an underwater chamber.

You take a deep breath and dive down. The passage is short, and you emerge in an air pocket—a dry chamber below the pool. And there, carefully wrapped in waterproof oilcloth: the treasure!
