Days pass.

With Doctor Claude gone and the Time Blenders destroyed, TIME CORP crumbles. The surviving henchmen flee the island on whatever boats they can find. Some surrender to the tribal warriors. Others simply vanish into the jungle, never to be seen again.

The volcano grows quiet. The lava cools. The Alamosaurus, remarkably, survives its fall. You see it sometimes, limping through the valley on injured legs, browsing peacefully on the tallest trees. The Quetzalcoatlus circles overhead, a living shadow against the sun.

Thea's tribe holds a feast in your honor. They paint your face with red ochre and drape flowers around your neck. Grumsh sits beside the fire, telling exaggerated tales of the battle to anyone who will listen. Your uncle laughs for the first time since the plane went down.

On the morning you leave, Thea walks you to the helipad. The TIME CORP helicopter sits waiting, its rotors still and silent. Your uncle is already inside, running through the preflight checklist.

"You will come back," Thea says. It is not a question.
